Day 3

Understanding the Network Inside Your Home

Today is about learning what your home network actually is, what your router does, how Wi-Fi connects your devices, and why this matters for basic cybersecurity.

Open Full Day 3 Lesson

What you will learn

Wi-Fi is not magic.

Your home network is made of real pieces: your router, modem, phones, laptops, TVs, game consoles, printers, and smart devices. The goal today is to stop feeling locked out and start understanding what is already running inside your home.

Core ideas

Day 3 Topics

Router

The traffic manager

Your router connects your devices, assigns addresses, manages traffic, and helps protect your home network.

Wi-Fi vs Internet

They are not the same

Wi-Fi connects your device to your router. The internet is the larger network outside your home.

IP Addresses

Device addresses

An IP address helps information know where to go, like a mailing address for a device on a network.

DNS

The internet phone book

DNS helps turn website names like example.com into addresses computers can understand.

Connected Devices

Know what exists

You cannot protect what you do not know exists. Day 3 includes your first simple home network inventory.

Security Basics

Start with awareness

You will learn where to check router admin passwords, Wi-Fi security, firmware updates, and guest network settings.

Safe practice

Day 3 Final Challenge

Before ending today, complete these simple steps:

  1. Find your router IP address.
  2. Open the router login page.
  3. Count how many devices are connected to your network.
  4. Write down at least five devices you recognize.
  5. Write down any devices you still need to identify.
  6. Check whether your Wi-Fi security uses WPA2 or WPA3.
  7. Write one sentence explaining what your router does.

This is not about becoming a networking expert in one day. This is about replacing intimidation with understanding.
